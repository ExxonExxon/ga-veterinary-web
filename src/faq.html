Everything you need to know

Common questions about custom veterinary surgical instruments, how I work, and how to get in touch.

What types of custom veterinary instruments do you design?

I design and manufacture a wide range of custom surgical instruments for wildlife, marine mammals, fish, and birds. This includes orthopaedic implants, fixation devices, laryngoscopes, surgical forceps, specialised needles, suture kits, and consumable devices. Every instrument is designed for the specific anatomy of the animal it will treat.

Do you create instruments for a single animal?

Yes, most of my projects are for a single animal or a small group. Whether it's a sun bear needing a custom pelvic implant, a dolphin requiring a fin corer, or a small bird needing micro fixation pins, I design and manufacture instruments for the individual animal in front of me.

How long does it take to design and manufacture a custom surgical device?

The timeline varies depending on complexity. Simple instruments can be designed and manufactured in a few weeks, while more complex devices like custom orthopaedic implants may take several months of design, prototyping, testing, and refinement.

What animals have you created instruments for?

I have created instruments for sun bears, whales, dolphins, sharks, kangaroos, wombats, koalas, kookaburras, turtles, and many other species. I specialise in animals that most medical device companies do not service because they are not commercially viable.

Do you work with zoos and wildlife hospitals internationally?

Yes, I work with zoos, wildlife hospitals, and veterinary organisations around the world. I have collaborated with teams in Australia, Borneo, Europe, and other regions to develop custom instruments for wildlife surgery.

How can I request a custom surgical device?

You can reach me via the contact form on this website, email me directly at info@gamedical.com.au, or call +61 (0) 421 238 399. Describe the animal and the surgical challenge, and I will work with you to develop a solution.

Do you provide on-ground support during surgeries?

Yes, where possible I sit in on procedures, assist veterinarians, and develop new instruments on the spot. Being present during surgeries helps me understand the challenges firsthand and design better instruments.

What materials do you use for your instruments?

I use medical-grade materials including surgical stainless steel, titanium (for rust-proof marine animal instruments), and other biocompatible materials. Each material is chosen for the specific requirements of the procedure and the animal.

How are your projects funded?

Most of my projects are self-funded. I donate a lot of my time to designing and developing devices for wildlife. Many devices never pay themselves off when you account for time, development, engineering, and production costs. Donations and sponsorships help me take on more projects.

How can I support GA Medical Veterinary's work?

You can support my work via the PayPal donation link on the website. Your contribution helps me develop vital medical devices for wildlife, marine mammals, fish, and birds that would otherwise go untreated.

Still have questions?

Get in touch and I will get back to you as soon as possible.
