Category 01

About My Work

I design and manufacture a wide range of custom surgical instruments for wildlife, marine mammals, fish, and birds. This includes orthopaedic implants, fixation devices, laryngoscopes, surgical forceps, specialised needles, suture kits, and consumable devices. Every instrument is designed for the specific anatomy of the animal it will treat.
Yes, most of my projects are for a single animal or a small group. Whether it's a sun bear needing a custom pelvic implant, a dolphin requiring a fin corer, or a small bird needing micro fixation pins, I design and manufacture instruments for the individual animal in front of me.
I have created instruments for sun bears, whales, dolphins, sharks, kangaroos, wombats, koalas, kookaburras, turtles, and many other species. I specialise in animals that most medical device companies do not service because they are not commercially viable.
Yes, I work with zoos, wildlife hospitals, and veterinary organisations around the world. I have collaborated with teams in Australia, Borneo, Europe, and other regions to develop custom instruments for wildlife surgery.
Category 02

Process & Collaboration

You can reach me via the contact form, email me directly at info@gamedical.com.au, or call +61 (0) 421 238 399. Describe the animal and the surgical challenge, and I will work with you to develop a solution.
To get started, I need details about the animal species, the specific medical condition or surgical challenge, and any existing imaging such as X-rays, CT scans, or measurements. If possible, photographs of the animal or the surgical site also help. The more information you can provide, the more precisely I can design the instrument for your needs.
Yes, absolutely. I regularly collaborate with veterinarians around the world without being physically present. I can design instruments from X-rays, CT scans, photographs, and detailed measurements sent to me remotely. Prototypes can then be shipped to you for testing and refinement. I'm accustomed to working across different time zones and will communicate with you throughout the process.
The timeline varies depending on complexity. Simple instruments can be designed and manufactured in a few weeks, while more complex devices like custom orthopaedic implants may take several months of design, prototyping, testing, and refinement. I will provide a realistic estimate after understanding your requirements.
Category 03

Manufacturing & Materials

I use medical-grade materials including surgical stainless steel, titanium (ideal for rust-proof marine animal instruments), and other biocompatible materials. Each material is chosen for the specific requirements of the procedure and the animal.
I manufacture most devices in-house, which gives me full control over quality, precision, and turnaround time. For specialised components or larger production runs, I partner with trusted Australian manufacturers who meet my quality standards. Every device, whether made in-house or with a partner, is personally inspected and tested before delivery.
Yes, where possible I sit in on procedures, assist veterinarians, and develop new instruments on the spot. Being present during surgeries helps me understand the challenges firsthand and design better instruments.
Because most of my work is custom-made for individual animals, I don't maintain a standard price list. However, I do have a catalogue of previously designed instruments and apparatus that you can request by contacting me. It will give you a sense of the range of work I do and what's possible.
Category 04

Funding & Support

Most of my projects are self-funded. I donate a lot of my time to designing and developing devices for wildlife. Many devices never pay themselves off when you account for time, development, engineering, and production costs. Donations and sponsorships help me take on more projects.
Costs vary significantly depending on complexity, materials, and development time. Simple modifications or single-purpose instruments are more affordable, while complex multi-component devices requiring prototyping and testing are naturally more involved. I'm transparent about pricing and will provide a quote after understanding your needs. I encourage you to get in touch to discuss your specific case.
You can support my work via the PayPal donation link. Your contribution helps me develop vital medical devices for wildlife, marine mammals, fish, and birds that would otherwise go untreated. Every bit helps, and I am grateful for your support.
